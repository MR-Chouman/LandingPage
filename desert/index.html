Sarra's World

The fairy who commands the desert — one storm, one dune, one blazing sun at a time.

— Midday Command

My blazing throne

I stare at the sun without blinking. We have an understanding — it burns so I don't have to. I ride its rays down to the sand each morning and dare the horizon to stop me.

— Morning Command

My shifting kingdom

Every morning I reshape my dunes. I push the sand with open palms and carve new ridges sharp as blades. My kingdom never looks the same twice — I made sure of that.

— My Fury

My war cry

When I spread my wings and spin, the storm obeys. I call it when the desert needs to remind the world of its power. Nothing stands against the sand when I command it.

— My Sentinels

My thorned guards

They answer to no one but me. I planted each one with my own hands, pressed them into the earth and told them — grow fierce, grow sharp. They never disappoint.

— Night Vigil

My ancient map

I named every star. I drew the constellations with my own wings, cutting trails of light across the black sky. The desert at night is mine alone — and the stars know better than to dim when I am watching.

— My Secret

My hidden mercy

I do not share this with anyone. The oasis is my one softness — hidden between dunes, cool and trembling. I come here alone to remember that power without tenderness is just destruction.

— A world divided

This is mine.

My sister rules the green world — soft and full of song, where everything bends toward her and blooms at her touch. We are opposites. Her rain would bury my dunes. My heat would scorch her flowers.

We love each other from a distance.
